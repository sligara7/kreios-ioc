Miscellaneous Tab

The Miscellaneous tab contains controls for measuring the beam power and calibrating the motors.

 

Faraday cup beam intensity

Beam power is measured by moving the anode to the Faraday cup position and measuring the beam current. This is then multiplied by the beam voltage to obtain power. To measure the beam power:

1. Select the preset you want to use.
2. Click Measure in the Miscellaneous tab. The motor will move to the Faraday cup position, then the source will start in order to measure the beam current. The result is then displayed in the Power field with a corresponding error.

 

Motor calibration

Motors are not fitted with encoders, so their position is unknown when the instrument is switched on. In order to make reproducible movements, the motor needs to be calibrated by moving it to a zero position.

 

NOTE:
At present, only the anode motor can be calibrated in SpecsLab Prodigy. The Bragg and focus motors can be calibrated on the front panel of the XRC-125 MF.

 

To calibrate the anode motor:

■    Click Anode Motor under Motor Calibration.
