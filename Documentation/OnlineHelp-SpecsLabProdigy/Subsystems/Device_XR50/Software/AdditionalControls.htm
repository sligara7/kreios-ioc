Additional Controls

By clicking the button, further controls and information are shown in the Device Control. There are four tabs which show different aspects of the source. Expand the sections below for more information.

 

Information

The Interlocks indicators show the status of the interlocks—green for safe, red for tripped:

■    Water—the water parameters (flow, pressure, temperature) are within the correct ranges. See the Limits tab. Note that this is red when the cooling water is switched off.

■    HV—the HV connector is fitted into the CCX 70, enabling the high voltage power supply in the UXC 1000.

■    Vacuum—the vacuum sensor connected to the rear of the UXC 1000 is in a safe condition.

■    RAP—the protective safety switch on the source (covering the anode flange) is connected correctly.

 

Device Mode shows the status of the source:

■    Off—filament current off, HV off, water off.

■    Standby—filament current at standby, HV off, water on.

■    Operate—filament current and HV at operating values.

 

Parameters for the cooling water show the status of the CCX 70. You can toggle the water cooling with the Start Cooling button. Note that the water cooling is started automatically when you switch the source on, and stopped when you click the Off button.

 

NOTE:
The water current is caused by the anode voltage drop over the cooling water. For stable operation, it should be in the range 2…10 mA. See the UXC 1000 manual for more information.

 

The Source parameters show the status of the operating parameters. You can also set the value of the emitter current. The values are shown for the emission current, anode current and number of arcs that have occurred during operation.

The arc limit is the number of arcs (fast discharges) that can occur during operation before the power supply switches off the source. See the UXC 1000 manual for more information about this and the other parameters.

Conditioning

After baking out the source, there are adsorbates on the working surfaces which can cause pressure bursts and arcing, thereby preventing stable operation. The conditioning procedures ramp up the operating voltages and currents so that the source is ready for operation.

 

Conditioning essentially involves going through the options in the Mode menu in sequence. Please see the UXC 1000 manual for more information. You need to apply the procedures to both anodes.

 

The following modes for conditioning are available:

■    Filament warmup—Ramps the filament up to the standby current.

■    Filament conditioning— Ramps up the filament current over a user-defined period to the filament current limit.

■    HV conditioning—Ramps up the anode voltage to its limit over a user-defined period and holds it there (steady state) for a given time.

■    Power conditioning—Slowly ramps up the currents and voltages until they are at the normal operating levels.

 

NOTE:
The filament warmup is not an essential part of the conditioning procedure—it is useful if you want to have the filament ready at the standby current ready for operation at a later time.
