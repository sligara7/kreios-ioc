Overview of Transmission Function

For quantitative analysis of samples, it is necessary to determine the exact areas under the curves in order to measure the ratios of different materials. The raw data can be deceptive in this case, since low energy electrons are transmitted through the analyzer with a lower efficiency than high energy electrons.

 

The peak position (or its shift from the standard value) is not affectedâ€”only the peak height (or area) changes. A peak at low energy may appear smaller than a peak at higher energies, even though there may be more material in the sample corresponding to the first peak.

 

The transmission function is a measure of transmission efficiency as a function of retarding ratio (i.e. Ek/Ep). It depends on a number of factors in the analyzer and experiment. In general, it is necessary to determine the energy transmission function for:

- Each lens mode

- Each combination of entrance and exit slits

- Each size of the excitation spot on the sample
